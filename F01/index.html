Welcome to Philippine Olympians: Legends of the Games

Whether you're a sports enthusiast, a proud supporter, or someone who believes in the power of dreams, we invite you to explore, celebrate, and take pride in the achievements of our Filipino Olympians. Together, lets honor their legacy and inspire the champions of tomorrow.

History

History of the Philippines in the Olympics

The Philippines holds a proud and historic legacy in the Olympic Games as the first Southeast Asian nation to compete, making its debut at the 1924 Paris Olympics. The country's first Olympic medal came in 1928, when Teófilo Yldefonso, known as the "Ilocano Shark," won bronze in the 200m breaststroke, becoming the first Asian swimmer to achieve such a feat. This success began a tradition of excellence, especially in sports like boxing, which saw Anthony Villanueva earn the nations first silver medal in the 1964 Tokyo Olympics. Boxing continued to shine with Mansueto “Onyok” Velasco, who won another silver in the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, solidifying the Philippines reputation as a force in combat sports.

The most significant milestone in Philippine Olympic history came during the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, when weightlifter Hidilyn Diaz made history as the first Filipino to win a gold medal, inspiring millions of Filipinos. That same Olympics also saw Nesthy Petecio and Carlo Paalam bring home silver medals in boxing, and Eumir Marcial secure a bronze in the mens middleweight division. With a total of 14 Olympic medals to date, the Philippines continues to nurture rising talents and prepare for future games, carrying forward its rich Olympic heritage and showcasing the passion and perseverance of Filipino athletes striving for greatness.

Upcoming Olympians and Rising Stars

Spotlight on the Future of Philippine Sports

EJ Obiena

Pole Vault

Age: 29
Hometown: Tondo, Manila
Notable Achievements:
- Current Asian record holder in pole vault (5.91m).
- Gold medalist in the 2023 Asian Games and multiple Diamond League podium finisher.
Olympic Goals: After finishing in the Top 10 at Tokyo 2020, EJ is determined to aim for the podium in the upcoming Paris 2024 Olympics.

Alex Eala

Tennis

Age: 19
Hometown: Quezon City
Notable Achievements:
- 2022 US Open Juniors Singles Champion.
- First Filipina to win a Grand Slam singles title in tennis.
Olympic Goals: With her rapid rise in the professional tennis rankings, Alex aims to qualify for her first Olympics and bring glory to the Philippines on the court.

Kyle Juane

Swimming

Age: 17
Hometown: Cebu City
Notable Achievements:
- Multiple gold medalist at the Southeast Asian Junior Swimming Championships.
- Broke the national junior record for the 100m freestyle.
Olympic Goals: Kyle is working toward qualifying for Paris 2024, focusing on freestyle and medley events.

Margielyn Didal

Skateboarding

Age: 26
Hometown: Cebu City
Notable Achievements:
- Gold medalist, 2018 Asian Games (Street Skateboarding).
- Top 7 finisher at Tokyo 2020 Olympics.
Olympic Goals: Margielyn is gearing up for another shot at Olympic glory, inspiring young Filipinos to take up skateboarding.
